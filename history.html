History of Volleyball

Origins

Volleyball was invented in 1895 by William G. Morgan, a physical education director in Holyoke, Massachusetts. It was designed as an indoor sport for businessmen who found basketball too vigorous.

Originally called "mintonette," the game quickly evolved and was renamed "volleyball" to describe how players volleyed the ball back and forth.

Early Development

The first official ball was specially designed for the sport in 1900. The rules were refined over the years, with the set and spike introduced in the Philippines in 1916.

By 1920, the three hits per side rule and back row attack rules were established.

Olympic Recognition

Volleyball became an Olympic sport in 1964, with both men's and women's competitions at the Tokyo Games. Beach volleyball was added to the Olympics in 1996.
