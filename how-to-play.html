How to Play Volleyball

Volleyball is played by two teams of six players on a court divided by a net. The objective is to make the ball touch the ground in the opponent's court. Each team can touch the ball up to three times before sending it over to the other side.

Basic Rules:

  • A team scores a point when the ball touches the ground in the opponent's court or when the opponent commits a fault.
  • The team that wins a rally scores a point and serves for the next point.
  • A match is typically played to the best of five sets. The first four sets are played to 25 points, and the deciding fifth set is played to 15 points.
  • A team must win a set by at least two points.
  • Players rotate positions clockwise when their team wins back the right to serve.

Court Types

Indoor Volleyball Court

The standard indoor volleyball court measures 18 meters (59 feet) long and 9 meters (29.5 feet) wide. The net height is 2.43 meters (7 feet 11 5/8 inches) for men and 2.24 meters (7 feet 4 1/8 inches) for women.

Beach Volleyball Court

A beach volleyball court is slightly smaller at 16 meters (52.5 feet) long and 8 meters (26.2 feet) wide. The playing surface is sand, which creates different dynamics compared to indoor play. The net height remains the same as indoor volleyball.

Grass/Outdoor Volleyball

Recreational or grass volleyball can be played on various surfaces including grass, concrete, or sand. Court dimensions may vary, but generally follow the standard measurements when possible.
